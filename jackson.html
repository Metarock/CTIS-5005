Jackson's Ethical Reflection

Ethical issues in relation to the effects of artificial intelligence using robotics in surgical health care are mainly seen to be the ever increasing autonomy of robotics and the developments of artificial intelligence which may in the future be able to guide these robotics through a complete surgical procedure. Thus ethical issues are going to arise on where the line in the sand is going to be drawn as to how much control over choices the AI will have when operating within a surgery. As there are certain rules conducting how robotics must interact with humanity such as not harming humans. Rules like this which the AI must follow may come into jeopardy when the robot must create incisions onto a person and begin surgery on them, also it may be difficult if unforeseen circumstances arise during surgery and the robot may be required to make a difficult decision between levels of pain for the human there may be a white line on how the robot should make that decision or if it even should be making the decision. My personal reflection is that in the near future, before AI and robotics have the ability to conduct a complete surgery autonomously, there must be new laws and legislations regarding these blurry ethical boundaries to ensure that the robotics installed are creating the most benefit possible for society. With little to no areas where the law may be exploited or unclear.

From the research which I have conducted, I recommend that through surgical robotics, there is never complete autonomy for the robotic AI to complete a whole surgery by itself. I believe that the robot should always be controlled by a human who makes the important decisions for the robot, while the robotics and AI may be made to adapt to different surgeons and how they operate rather than to conduct a surgery by themselves. This way, there will always be a person who is liable for the decisions made while conducting surgery on another human being. While the robotics will continue to just aid and make the surgeons life easier rather than replacing many peoples job which they have spent countless hours studying for.
